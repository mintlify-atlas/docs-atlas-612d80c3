Every listing on TruequeU is assigned to one of four categories. Choosing the right category makes your listing easier to find and sets buyer expectations correctly.

## Electronics

Electronics is the most active category on TruequeU. It covers any powered device or tech accessory used for studying, communication, or productivity.

**Example items:**
- Laptop (MacBook, ThinkPad, Surface)
- Smartphone
- Scientific calculator (TI-84, Casio fx-991)
- USB-C hub or multiport adapter
- Noise-isolating headphones
- Wireless mouse or mechanical keyboard
- 24-inch monitor
- Laptop stand or laptop bag

**Tips for electronics listings:**

- State the exact model number and storage/RAM specs where relevant (e.g., *"MacBook Air M2, 8GB RAM, 256GB SSD"*).
- Note the condition honestly — `Like New`, `Good`, or `Worn`. Buyers will test the item in person.
- List every accessory included: original box, charger, case, stylus.
- For items with batteries (laptops, phones), mention battery health if you know it.
- Price electronics by checking recent resale values for that model online before listing.

<Tip>
  Electronics listings with a real photo — not a manufacturer image — receive more buyer inquiries. Place the item on a plain surface and photograph it in natural light.
</Tip>

## Books

Textbooks are one of the most common items exchanged between students. The Books category covers anything academic: required course texts, reference guides, study prep books, and supplementary reading.

**Example items:**
- Calculus textbook (Stewart, Thomas)
- Introductory Python or programming textbook
- Organic chemistry reference guide
- LSAT / GRE / MCAT prep book
- Course packet or printed lecture notes

**Tips for books listings:**

- Include the full title, author, and edition in the listing title (e.g., *"Calculus Stewart 8th Edition"*). Edition number is critical — buyers often need a specific one.
- In the description, disclose the extent of highlighting, written notes, or missing pages.
- Mention whether the book includes access codes (and if those codes have already been used).
- Price used textbooks at 30–60% of the new retail price, depending on condition.

<Warning>
  Access codes for online platforms (Pearson, McGraw-Hill, WileyPLUS) are single-use. If the code has been redeemed, state this clearly in the description to avoid disputes.
</Warning>

## Services

The Services category is unique to TruequeU's peer marketplace model. Instead of a physical item, you are offering your time and expertise to other students.

**Example listings:**
- Calculus or linear algebra tutoring (per hour)
- Essay or thesis proofreading
- Airport or train station transportation (per trip)
- Photography for student projects or events
- Spanish / Mandarin / French language practice sessions

**Tips for service listings:**

- Be specific about what you offer, how long a session lasts, and your rate. Vague listings get fewer responses.
- State your qualifications briefly — your major, course grades, or relevant experience.
- Mention your availability (e.g., *"Available weekday evenings and weekends"*).
- Set the price per unit (per hour, per essay, per trip) so buyers can calculate total cost upfront.
- Use the description field to explain your process: how you handle revisions, what materials you need the buyer to provide, and your preferred meeting format (in-person or remote).

<Note>
  Service listings use the same status system as physical items. Mark your listing `reserved` when you are fully booked, and `available` again when your schedule opens up.
</Note>

## Other

The Other category is a catch-all for anything that does not fit Electronics, Books, or Services. It is well-suited for dorm and apartment items that students need at the start and end of each academic year.

**Example items:**
- Desk lamp or floor lamp
- Office chair or desk
- Mini fridge or microwave
- Whiteboard
- Sports equipment (bicycle, yoga mat, gym bag)
- Clothing (jackets, uniforms, formal wear)
- Dorm supplies (shelving, hangers, bedding)

**Tips for Other listings:**

- Include dimensions for furniture so buyers know whether the item fits their space.
- For appliances, state the wattage or voltage if relevant, and whether the item is in full working order.
- Clothing listings should include the size, brand, and any wear or staining.
- Mention whether you can help with transport for large items like chairs or shelving units.

<Tip>
  Demand for furniture and appliances spikes at the start and end of each semester. List early in August and December to reach the most buyers.
</Tip>
