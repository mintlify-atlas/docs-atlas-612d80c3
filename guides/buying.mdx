TruequeU connects you with fellow university students selling items, offering services, and open to trades. This guide walks you through finding what you need and closing a deal safely on campus.

## Finding items

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search by keyword">
    Use the search bar at the top of the page to filter listings by title. The search runs instantly across all active listings — both community-posted and seeded items. Try terms like `calculus`, `TI-84`, or `monitor`.

    <Tip>
      Short, specific keywords work best. Searching `python book` returns more relevant results than `introductory programming textbook for beginners`.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Browse by category">
    When creating a listing, sellers assign one of four categories: **Electronics**, **Books**, **Services**, or **Other**. Use these categories to narrow your search when you have a general need rather than a specific item in mind.

    <Note>
      The listings page shows all categories together. Use the search bar alongside a category keyword (e.g., `electronics headphones`) to filter further.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the listings page">
    The main listings page at `/` shows all available, reserved, and sold items. Each listing card displays the title, price, category, and a thumbnail image. Scroll through to spot items that match your needs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading a listing

Click any listing card to open its detail page. Here is what each field means:

| Field | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Status badge** | `available` — ready to trade. `reserved` — seller is in talks with someone. `sold` — no longer available. |
| **Price** | The seller's asking price in USD. This is a starting point, not necessarily final. |
| **Category** | The broad type of item or service. |
| **Description** | Condition, reason for selling, trade preferences, and any extras included. |
| **Seller info** | The seller's display name and university domain derived from their email. |

<Note>
  A `reserved` status means the seller is negotiating with another buyer, but the deal is not final. You can still reach out — the seller may accept multiple inquiries.
</Note>

## Contacting a seller

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your account">
    You must be logged in to contact a seller. If you are not, the **Contact Seller** button redirects you to `/login`. After logging in, return to the listing detail page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start a chat from the listing detail page">
    On the listing detail page, click the **Contact Seller** button or the **Contact** link in the seller card. This opens a dedicated chat thread at `/chat/{listingId}-{yourUserId}` and automatically creates the conversation if it does not already exist.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send your first message">
    Once in the chat, type your message in the input field and click **Send** (or press Enter). A good opening message introduces yourself and states your interest clearly.

    <Tip>
      Example opening: *"Hi! I'm interested in the TI-84 calculator. Is it still available? Would you accept $50?"*
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Negotiating and arranging a trade

All negotiation happens inside the chat thread. There is no formal offer system — communication is direct and informal, which keeps things fast.

- **State your offer clearly.** Include the amount or trade item you are proposing.
- **Use the Make an Offer button** on the listing detail page to signal interest before opening a full chat.
- **Agree on a meeting point.** Campus libraries, student union buildings, and cafeterias are common handoff spots.
- **Confirm details in writing.** Use the chat to confirm the final price, item condition, and meeting time before showing up.

<Note>
  TruequeU does not process payments. All transactions — cash, bank transfer, or item swap — are arranged directly between buyer and seller.
</Note>

## Tips for safe campus transactions

<Tip>
  Meet in a public, well-lit campus location during daytime hours. The library entrance or student services building are good defaults.
</Tip>

- **Inspect the item before paying.** For electronics, power it on. For books, check for missing pages or heavy highlighting.
- **Bring exact change or agree on a payment method in advance.** Avoid last-minute surprises.
- **Do not share personal contact details until you are confident about the seller.** The in-app chat is sufficient for all pre-sale communication.
- **Check the seller's profile.** Their university email domain is visible on the listing detail page — students from the same institution are easier to verify.
- **Trust your instincts.** If a deal feels off, walk away. There will be another listing.
